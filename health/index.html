Effects

Cocaine

In short, Cocaine is a deadly substance. It releases too much dopamine, which dammages your ability to do that natrually. It causes your body to work overtime, which caues many issues in your circlatory system

Heroin

Heroin is an extremly addictive drug. This drug is also classified as an opoid. This drug can cause many things, such as brain damage, heart damage, and lung damage. Even though this drug is made from beatiful flowers, it can and will harm you in lethal ways. To learn more about the effects of this drug, click below.

Methamphetamine

Meth can be smoked, sniffed or injected. However, it should not be. Most of this is made illigally and secretly imported from other countries. Meth is very harmful to your body. This leads to the 'meth mouth' issue. This subtance can often cause memory loss and unhealthy weight loss. Click below to learn more.

Ecstasy

Ectasy is known as 'molly' or in the medical world MDMA. This is a very synthetic drug. This drug messes with the mind and the body. Long term use can lead to blood issues leading to organ failure and or death.

Marijana

Marijana is dried parts of Cannabis sativa or Cannabis indica plants. This drug is the most addictive one used after alcohol and tabacoo. THC is the chemical from Marijana that affects your brain. This chemical affects your brain in many different ways.

LSD

LSD is a hallucinogenic drug. These types of drugs are really evil. They can create places in your mind. They make hallucenations. They make you think and feel things that aren't really there. Sometimes, they can take you on 'trips'. Sometimes these trips are enjoyable, but sometiems they are a nightmare. They can even end in death. Hallucinogenic is a very VERY nasty substance.






Recovery

Let's face it. Drugs are evil. To combat this you will need help from God. It's like math (algebra, specifically) when solving for x, you do the inverse of opperations. If x is smothered in evilness, then give it love, hope and blessings from God, and your problems with drugs can be solved.

This is often called the 12-step program. If you follow the steps correctly, you can obtain freedom from your addiction.

  1. Admitting powerlessness over the addiction
  2. Believing that God can help
  3. Deciding to turn control over to God
  4. Taking a personal inventory
  5. Admitting to God, oneself, and another person the wrongs done
  6. Being ready to have God correct any shortcomings in one’s character
  7. Asking God to remove those shortcomings
  8. Making a list of wrongs done to others and being willing to make amends for those wrongs
  9. Contacting those who have been hurt, unless doing so would harm the person
  10. Continuing to take personal inventory and admitting when one is wrong
  11. Seeking his will and connection with God via prayer and meditation
  12. Carrying the message of the 12 Steps to others in need
